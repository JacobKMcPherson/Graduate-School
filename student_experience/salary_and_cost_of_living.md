# Salary & Cost of Living for PhD Students

## Overview

PhD stipends vary widely — from below $18,000 to over $45,000 per year — while the cost of living in university cities ranges just as dramatically. A stipend that sounds reasonable in one city may leave students unable to afford rent, food, or transportation in another. This guide helps you evaluate your real purchasing power and advocate for fair compensation.

---

## 1. Key Questions to Ask Before Accepting an Offer

- [ ] What is the **annual stipend** (pre-tax)?
- [ ] Is the stipend a **fellowship**, a **TA/RA appointment**, or a combination? (Each has different tax treatment.)
- [ ] Does the stipend include a **tuition waiver and fee coverage**? Are mandatory student fees covered?
- [ ] How often is the stipend **reviewed or increased** (e.g., annually, tied to NIH NRSA rates)?
- [ ] Is the stipend **12-month** or does it require summer funding separately?
- [ ] What are the **cost-of-living adjustments** built into multi-year offers?

## 3. Adjusting for City Cost of Living

To compare stipends across cities, use the **Cost of Living Index (COLI)**. The table below gives approximate COLI values (US average = 100).

| City | Approx. COLI | Implication for a $30,000 stipend |
|---|---|---|
| Rural Midwest | 85 | Equivalent to ~$35,300 purchasing power |
| Austin, TX | 100 | Face value |
| Seattle, WA | 115 | Equivalent to ~$26,100 purchasing power |
| Boston, MA | 125 | Equivalent to ~$24,000 purchasing power |
| New York City, NY | 145 | Equivalent to ~$20,700 purchasing power |
| San Francisco, CA | 155 | Equivalent to ~$19,400 purchasing power |

**Formula:**  
`Adjusted Purchasing Power = Stipend × (100 / Local COLI)`

- [NerdWallet Cost of Living Calculator](https://www.nerdwallet.com/cost-of-living-calculator)
- [MIT Living Wage Calculator](https://livingwage.mit.edu/)
- [BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey](https://www.bls.gov/cex/)

## 4. Negotiating Your Stipend

Most PhD students don't realize stipends are negotiable — especially at offer time. Here are evidence-based strategies:

### Before negotiating
- [ ] Research peer institutions' stipend levels (most are publicly posted).
- [ ] Look up your city's MIT Living Wage and bring it to the conversation.
- [ ] Check whether your program has recently received new training grant funding (T32, NRSA), which can increase what PIs and programs can offer.
- [ ] Ask your potential advisor about supplemental RA funding, travel awards, and conference support.

### At the negotiation
- [ ] Frame the ask around cost of living, not personal need: *"I've researched that the living wage in [city] is $X, and I want to make sure I can focus fully on research."*
- [ ] Ask about guaranteed annual increases: *"Is there a policy for annual stipend adjustments, and if so, what is the typical percentage?"*
- [ ] If the stipend can't be raised, ask for alternatives: summer support, fee waivers, childcare subsidies, or professional development funds.

### After accepting
- [ ] Set a calendar reminder to revisit your stipend each year relative to inflation (CPI) and local cost-of-living changes.
- [ ] Advocate collectively through your graduate student union or student government for program-wide increases.

## 5. Taxes on Graduate Stipends

Stipend taxation depends on how you are funded:

| Funding Type | Federal Tax | FICA (Social Security + Medicare) |
|---|---|---|
| Fellowship / scholarship (F31, NSF GRF) | Yes — you must pay estimated quarterly taxes | Generally exempt |
| TA / RA appointment (W-2) | Yes — withheld automatically | Generally exempt if enrolled half-time |
| Industry-funded RA | Depends on contract terms | May apply |

> **Action:** File Form 1040-ES quarterly to avoid underpayment penalties if you receive a fellowship. See IRS Publication 970.

**Resources:**
- [IRS Publication 970: Tax Benefits for Education](https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/p970.pdf)
- [GradTaxes.com](https://www.gradtaxes.com/) — tax guides specifically for graduate students
- Your university's international student office (if applicable) for treaty exemptions
