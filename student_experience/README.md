# PhD Student Experience Resources

This directory provides practical guides for PhD students covering financial wellness, health, family, and career planning — areas that are often under-supported by academic programs but critical to long-term success and well-being.

## Contents

| Guide | Topic |
|---|---|
| [Salary & Cost of Living](salary_and_cost_of_living.md) | Evaluate whether your stipend covers local living costs; tools for negotiation |
| [Wealth Building](wealth_building.md) | Budgeting, emergency funds, retirement contributions, and tax strategy |
| [Family Planning](family_planning.md) | Parental leave policies, childcare, partner career considerations |
| [Health Insurance & Mental Health](health_insurance.md) | Understanding coverage, navigating mental health resources and crises |
| [Career ROI, Contracts & Employment Prospects](career_roi.md) | Calculating the value of a PhD, reviewing offer letters, job search strategy |

## How to Use These Resources

Each guide is structured as:
1. **Key questions to ask** — What you should know before signing or committing.
2. **Reference benchmarks** — Industry standards, federal minimums, and peer comparisons.
3. **Action items / checklists** — Concrete steps you can take today.
4. **External links** — Curated tools, calculators, and advocacy organizations.

These documents are living templates — fork the repository, customize them for your institution and situation, and update them as policies change.
